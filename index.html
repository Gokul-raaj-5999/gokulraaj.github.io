1. Delhivery - EDA

Data analysis reveals trends in delivery patterns for the year 2018, particularly in September and October, although caution is advised due to the relatively small dataset size. Missing values in source and destination centers were identified and dropped, with attempts made to reconcile using existing data but proved inconclusive. Columns with undefined properties were dropped for code clarity and reusability. Data normalization was conducted to standardize courier routes and analyze trip segments efficiently. Analysis of destination centers revealed concentration in specific states, suggesting targeted infrastructure improvements for efficiency. Similar operations were conducted for source states, indicating potential optimization opportunities. Trip creation times exhibited patterns, suggesting targeted order placement times for efficiency. Analysis of scan times indicated efficient delivery processes with minimal delays.

Further comparisons between actual and estimated delivery times highlighted areas for potential optimization. Outlier treatment revealed differences in mean delivery times, suggesting segmentation improves efficiency. Analysis of distances showed optimization in route planning, reducing overall travel distance. Chi-square analysis revealed independence among source and destination variables, limiting predictive inference. ANOVA analysis indicated variations in mean distances between different delivery routes. High trip creation rates in specific states point to areas of high delivery demand. Overall, data-driven recommendations focus on optimizing delivery processes and infrastructure to enhance efficiency and meet demand effectively.
